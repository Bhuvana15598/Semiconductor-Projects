AlGaN/GaN Polarization-Induced 2DEG Density Simulation
Overview:

This project investigates the formation of a two-dimensional electron gas (2DEG) in AlGaN/GaN heterostructures. The simulation estimates how the aluminum composition in AlGaN influences polarization-induced interface charge and the resulting electron density at the AlGaN/GaN interface.

Objective:

The goal is to study the relationship between aluminum mole fraction and 2DEG density using established polarization theory for III-nitride semiconductors. Understanding this relationship is important for the design of high-electron-mobility transistors (HEMTs), quantum devices, and advanced semiconductor structures.

Methodology:

The model evaluates:

1. AlGaN material properties vs. Al content - implied
2. Strain on GaN substrate - implied intermediate step.
3. Spontaneous polarization — blue curve.
4. Piezoelectric polarization —  orange curve.
5. Total polarization charge at interface — green curve.

The results are presented through plots showing polarization contributions and carrier density variation with aluminum composition.

The simulation demonstrates that increasing the aluminum fraction increases the polarization discontinuity at the interface. This leads to higher sheet charge density and consequently a higher 2DEG concentration. The trend agrees with established theoretical and experimental studies of AlGaN/GaN heterostructures.

Applications:

1. GaN HEMTs
2. Semiconductor quantum devices
3. High-frequency electronics
4. Wide-bandgap semiconductor research
5. Polarization-engineered heterostructures
